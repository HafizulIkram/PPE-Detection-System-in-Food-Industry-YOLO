Background

Personal Protective Equipment in Food Industry

The use of personal protective equipment (PPE) in the food industry is crucial for safeguarding both customers and employees from health risks by creating a barrier against contaminants. Despite frequent inspections by health authorities, food poisoning remains a significant issue, as evidenced by the 18.40% incidence rate in 2021. PPE, including hair nets, masks, gloves, aprons, and shoes, is essential to prevent cross-contamination.

A study of food handlers in Malaysia revealed varying compliance rates, with an average of 75% wearing aprons, 78% using hair restraints, 64% wearing gloves, and 74.5% wearing covered shoes. The study highlighted that a notable percentage of workers still neglect proper PPE usage, underscoring the need for improved compliance and the potential benefit of implementing an object detection system to monitor PPE use in the food industry.
